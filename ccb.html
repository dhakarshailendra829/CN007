(G) gajar ka Halwa with Dry Fruits Logout


Ingredients:
  • 500 grams carrots (around 4-5 medium carrots)
    		•
  • 1 cup full-fat milk
    		•
  • ¾ cup sugar
    		•
  • 4 tbsp ghee
    		•
  • ¼ cup chopped cashews
    		•
  • ¼ cup chopped raisins
    		•
  • ¼ cup chopped almonds
    		•
  • ¼ cup chopped pistachios (optional)
    		•
  • 10 green cardamoms, pods cracked
    		•
  • Pinch of saffron (optional)
    		•
  • ½ tsp ground cardamom powder
    		•
Method:
Step 1: Grate the Carrots
Wash, peel, and grate the carrots using a box grater or food processor. You can also use a juicer and scrape the carrot pulp.

Step 2: Cook the Carrots
Heat 2 tablespoons of ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan or kadai over medium heat. Add the grated carrots and cook, stirring occasionally, for 5-7 minutes, until the carrots soften slightly and release their moisture.

Step 3: Add Milk and Sugar
Add the full-fat milk and ¾ cup of sugar to the pan. Stir well to combine. Increase the heat to medium-high and bring the mixture to a boil.

Step 4: Simmer and Reduce Milk
Reduce the heat to low and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the milk reduces by about half and the mixture thickens. This can take 20-30 minutes, depending on the amount of moisture in the carrots.

Step 5: Add Dry Fruits and Spices
While the milk is reducing, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of ghee in a small pan over medium heat. Add the cashews, raisins, almonds, and pistachios (if using). Fry for a minute or two, until the nuts are lightly golden brown.
Once the milk has reduced and the halwa has thickened, add the fried dry fruits, cardamom pods, and ground cardamom powder. Stir well to combine.
(Optional) If using saffron, soak a pinch of saffron strands in 1 tablespoon of warm milk for 10 minutes. Then add the saffron-infused milk to the halwa mixture.

Step 6: Simmer and Adjust Consistency
Continue to simmer the halwa for another 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it reaches your desired consistency. The halwa should be thick and slightly loose, not dry and crumbly. You can test the consistency by pressing a spoon into the halwa. If a clean line forms behind your spoon, the halwa is done.

Step 7: Garnish and Serve
Remove the halwa from the heat and garnish with additional chopped nuts and a sprinkle of cardamom powder (optional). Serve the gajar ka halwa hot or warm. It tastes even better at room temperature or chilled. Enjoy!
