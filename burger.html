Method:
**Dal (Lentils)**
Step 1 - Wash and Soak Dal
Wash the tuar dal thoroughly in water. Soak it in enough water for at least 4 hours, or overnight.

Step 2 - Pressure Cook Dal
Drain the soaked dal and add it to a pressure cooker with 4 cups of water, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala powder, salt, chopped ginger, cloves, and bay leaf. Pressure cook for 4-5 whistles or until the dal is tender. Let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes before opening the lid.
Step 3 - Temper the Dal
In a pan, heat ghee over medium heat. Add the cooked dal and mash slightly with a spoon. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the dal reaches desired consistency. You can adjust the consistency by adding more water or mashing the dal further.
Garnish with chopped coriander leaves before serving.

**Baati (Wheat Balls)**
Step 1 - Make the Dough
In a large bowl, combine whole wheat flour, curd, salt, and ghee. Mix well using your hands to form a stiff dough. Gradually add water, a tablespoon at a time, until you get a smooth and slightly firm dough that doesn't stick to your hands.
Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until it becomes elastic and smooth. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2 - Shape the Baati
Pinch off small balls from the dough and roll them between your palms to form smooth balls. Make sure there are no cracks on the surface.
Poke a small hole in the center of each ball with your thumb. This will help the baati cook evenly from inside.

Step 3 - Cook the Baati
There are two ways to cook the baati:
**Method 1: Tandoor**
Preheat your tandoor. Arrange the baati on a skewer or baking tray and place them inside the tandoor. Cook for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown and cooked through, turning occasionally.
**Method 2: Oven**
Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). Arrange the baati on a baking tray lined with parchment paper. Brush the baati with melted ghee. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and cooked through, turning halfway through.

**Churma (Sweet Crumbled Bread)**
Step 1 - Crumble the Baati
Once the baati are cooked and cool, break them into small pieces. You can use a mortar and pestle or your hands to crumble them into a coarse mixture.

Step 2 - Roast the Churma
Heat ghee in a pan over medium heat. Add the crumbled baati and roast for 5-7 minutes, stirring constantly, until they become slightly golden brown and crisp.

Step 3 - Sweeten the Churma
Add powdered sugar and chopped nuts to the roasted baati mixture. Mix well to combine. You can adjust the sweetness according to your preference.
(Optional) Add a pinch of cardamom powder for an extra flavor boost.

Step 4 - Serve
Serve hot dal with baati and churma. Enjoy this delicious Rajasthani dish!
